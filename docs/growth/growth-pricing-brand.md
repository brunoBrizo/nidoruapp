## Growth Thesis

The product has built-in short-form potential because breathwork is visible, time-bound, and easy to try while watching a video. The growth strategy should show the product working in real time rather than explain it abstractly.

The app's strongest shareable surfaces are:

- Breathing pacer animation.
- Evening wind-down transition into sound.
- Ambient mixer.
- Session result card.
- 7-day challenge progress.
- Sleep insight card after enough check-ins.

## Repeatable Content Formats

### 1. Watch Me Fall Asleep In 10 Minutes

Use a screen recording of the evening wind-down flow with a timer overlay.

Hook:

- "I used to scroll for 45 minutes. Tonight I tried this instead."

Product requirement:

- The wind-down flow must be visually understandable in a recording.

### 2. Try This Right Now

Creator follows the breathing pacer for 30-60 seconds.

Hook:

- "If your mind is busy before bed, breathe with this for one minute."

Product requirement:

- Pacer animation must be smooth and readable even without audio.

### 3. Day X Challenge

A 7-day arc with sleep rating, bedtime routine, and one visible change.

Hook:

- "Day 3 of no-scroll bedtime."

Product requirement:

- Challenges need simple progress state and shareable screenshots.

### 4. Technique Explainers

Short educational clips about 4-7-8, box breathing, coherent breathing, and sleep routines.

Hook:

- "Why this slow breathing rhythm can help a bedtime routine feel calmer."

Product requirement:

- Keep in-app and public technique explanations clear, non-clinical, and aligned with the technique audit. Avoid "treats anxiety," "cures insomnia," "activates your parasympathetic nervous system," "guaranteed sleep," and similar claims.

### 5. Late-Night Cannot Sleep

Post and advertise during late-night windows.

Hook:

- "If you are reading this at 1 AM, try this with me."

Product requirement:

- App store page and first launch must match that immediate rescue promise.

## Viral Product Requirements

Do not treat growth as only marketing. The product must create assets worth sharing:

- Pacer replay or session card in a clean dark visual style.
- A short result summary after each breath session.
- A comeback badge instead of a shame reset.
- A challenge progress card at Day 1, Day 3, and Day 7.
- Insight cards that are cautious but personal.

## Pricing Architecture

Initial model:

| Plan | Price | Includes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Free | $0 | Core breathing pacer, 3 ambient sounds, morning check-in, limited history |
| Monthly | $7.99/month | Full sound library, all techniques, routines, insights, stories when available |
| Annual | $39.99/year | Same as monthly, positioned as the best value |
| Lifetime | $79.99 one-time | Offer selectively, not as the default first-session choice |

Pricing rationale:

- The annual price sits at the upper end of the source blueprint's acceptable range.
- Monthly is high enough to make annual attractive.
- Lifetime exists for subscription-anxious users.
- The free tier must remain useful because it drives sharing.

## Trial Strategy

Recommended first test after the first completed session:

- 14-day full-access trial.
- No credit card required if technically and commercially feasible.
- Day 14 insight recap as conversion moment.
- After trial, fall back to a real free tier instead of locking the app.

If no-credit-card trials are not supported by the chosen store flow, the alternative is:

- Free first session.
- Soft account creation after value.
- Paid trial only after the user sees the full routine value.

Later pricing experiments can test a shorter 7-day trial, but the current default is 14 days because it gives the insight engine enough data to show a stronger two-week progress pattern while still keeping the commitment limited.

## Renewal Trust Policy

Send renewal reminders before annual renewal:

- 7 days before renewal where platform rules allow.
- Date of renewal.
- Amount.
- Direct cancel link or clear instructions.

Billing UX requirements:

- Cancel path is Profile -> Subscription -> Cancel.
- Offer a pause option before final cancellation, but do not create a loop.
- State the refund policy on the subscription screen.
- Show the annual total anywhere monthly equivalent is shown.
- Do not use "just $X/day" framing without the annual price beside it.

This should be treated as brand strategy, not only support hygiene. The product should win trust against competitors that users describe as billing-hostile.

## Brand Direction

Brand should feel:

- Quiet.
- Honest.
- Nightly.
- Practical.
- Human.
- Calm without being generic.

Avoid:

- Medical authority posture.
- Celebrity-first positioning.
- Heavy spirituality language.
- Productivity optimization language.
- Dark-pattern subscription copy.
